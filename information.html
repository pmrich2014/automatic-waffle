The Ammortization Formula

Amortization refers to paying off debt amount on periodically over time till loan principle reduces to zero. Amount paid monthly is known as EMI which is equated monthly installment. EMI has both principal and interest component in it which is calculated by amortization formula. Amortization calculation depends on the principle, the rate of interest and time period of the loan.


\[mc = {rP \over n[1 - (1 + \frac r n)^{-nt}]}\]


\(P\) Principal is the amount of the loan borrowed to cover project
\(t\) The length of the loan or duration of the project in years.
\(r\) The proportion of the loan that is charged as interest annualy.
\(n\) Number of payments to be made in a year
\(mc\) The amount of money paid monthly to cover loan with interest.


Ammortization: Annual Project Cost Formula

Annual Costs = Monthly Cost * 12 Months

To calculate our annual project costs our calculation computes the annual cost of a project that is funded by a loan with a fixed interest rate over a fixed number of years.*

*Note: Our calucation assumes the project is 100% funded by the loan and that the project time line is equal to the loan term.



An Example

If your project cost is $150,000 fully funded by a loan for 10 years with an interest rate of 4%, then your annual costs will be $18,224.16.

Annual Cost: \($18,224.16 = ($1,518.68)(12)\)


Monthly Cost: \($1,518.68 = {(0.04)($150,000) \over 12[1 - (1 + \frac{0.04}{12})^{-(12)(10)}]}\)
